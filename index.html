About Me

I was born on September 11, 1999 in Mexico City. When I was one year old, my family immigrated to United States. My family struggled at first, moving from place to place. Eventually in 2006, we settled in a town in Idaho called Roberts where the population is about 500 people. The small town provided a perfect night sky that sparked my interest in Astronomy. Being immigrants, my family faced many hardships; however, we worked hard so that my sister and I could attend college. I attained Differed Action for Childhood Arrivals (DACA) immigration status when I turned 16. This allowed me to get a job, but it did not permit me to receive any Federal money. Subsequently, I cannot obtain majority of scholarships. Also, my parents do not have the funds to send me to college, but I was awarded the Dream.US Opportunity scholarship that provided DACA students with full rides to various schools including Delaware State University. Currently, I am in my final year at DSU where I have been able to continue my love for playing soccer recreationally. I am a massive FC Barcelona fan. I also enjoy collecting music records and hiking.
